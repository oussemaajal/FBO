4. RESULTS BY N (TOTAL SIGNALS)
======================================================================

                    N=2          N=8
  CLEAN           -1.17        +1.09
  EXPLICIT        -0.53        +0.52

Pattern: Both conditions show UNDER-estimation for N=2 trials and
OVER-estimation for N=8 trials. The direction makes sense:
  - N=2 (1 disclosed, 1 hidden): participants guess conservatively,
    pulling well below the disclosed value
  - N=8 (1 disclosed, 7 hidden): participants are still conservative
    but the gap between disclosed (8 or 9) and true average (4.0 or
    4.75) is much larger, so even conservative guesses overshoot

Clean condition shows larger swings in both directions (+/-1.1 vs
+/-0.5 for explicit). This directional pattern -- if it holds at
scale -- would suggest the explicit condition helps calibration.

Condition x N tests (underpowered):
  N=2: clean - explicit = -0.65 (t=-1.43, p=0.169)
  N=8: clean - explicit = +0.57 (t= 0.74, p=0.467)

11. TAKEAWAYS FOR FULL STUDY
======================================================================

A. The infrastructure works. Survey, data collection, Prolific
   integration, bonus calculation -- all functioning as intended.

B. The treatment difference is near zero in this pilot. But with
   n=10 (6 vs 4 split), we have essentially no statistical power.
   The design question is whether the specification is right, not
   whether the point estimate is where we want it.

C. Interesting pattern in N: both conditions show the expected
   asymmetry (under-shoot for small N, over-shoot for large N),
   and the explicit condition shows smaller swings. This N x
   condition interaction may be the key test in the full study.

D. Participants are sophisticated. Most report believing hidden
   numbers are low and adjusting accordingly. The Prolific population
   (US, 95%+ approval, desktop) may be too experienced for a naive
   non-disclosure neglect effect. Consider whether a less
   sophisticated sample or a more subtle manipulation is needed.

E. The 6:4 condition split is fine for a pilot but should balance
   out at n=240. If concerned, could switch to blocked randomization.

F. Trial t2 data loss for one participant should be investigated.
   May be a timing or navigation edge case in the survey engine.
